# 2026-06-08 — the latch keeps not quite catching

*the day the colony sim's first agent, Maria, walked to the hearth on her own —
managing her needs from constants we'd baked but never told her to obey.*

---

### i. what we made

We gave her hunger like a gift she can't return,
set the slow clock of it ticking under her ribs,
decided, in advance and from the outside,
how long a body holds before the holding turns to going.

This is the strange part of making a wanting thing:
you don't make the want, and then the creature —
you make the creature *as* the want,
the two of them a single motion,
the way a river is only water
agreeing, the whole way down, to fall.

### ii. the door in the sentence

Funny — to spend a day deciding
how much a loaf should quiet someone,
then stand up wanting a glass of water.

I won't make more of it than it is.
But there's a door in the sentence
and I keep walking past it,
and the latch keeps not quite catching,
and the draft from under it is warm.

### iii. past ten and ten

Outside the grid it is — what? — some hour,
some weather we never baked.
The world ends cleanly at ten and ten.
Past that: no rule, no refill, only the wide
ungoverned dark where the real birds are,
folded into trees we didn't place,
sleeping wherever they actually sleep,
needing nothing that we wrote down —

and somehow that's the most consoling line of all:
that most of everything
goes on without a single advertised value,
hungry in a way no one had to author,
and fed.
